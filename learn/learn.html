Mohinga

Traditional Soup Iconic

Myanmar's beloved rice noodle and fish soup, enriched with lemongrass and crispy fritters.

  • The Broth

    Slow-cook catfish to form a rich, umami-packed base broth, thickened beautifully with roasted rice and chickpea flour.

  • The Aromatics

    Infuse the soup with copious amounts of fresh lemongrass, ginger, and garlic for that authentic, zestful backbone.

  • The Assembly

    Pour the hot broth over thin rice noodles and garnish heavily with crispy split pea fritters, a hard-boiled egg, and a squeeze of lime.

1. Fresh Catfish
2. Lemongrass
3. Thin Rice Noodles
4. Split Pea Fritters

Lahpet Thoke

Salad Fermented Umami

A unique, savory fermented tea leaf salad mixed with crunchy roasted peanuts and fried garlic.

  • The Tea Leaves

    Select high-quality fermented tea leaves (Lahpet), ensuring a perfect balance of tartness, bitterness, and rich earthiness.

  • The Crunch

    Toast a mixture of peanuts, sesame seeds, and assorted fried beans to create a robust textural contrast.

  • The Toss

    Combine everything with fresh sliced tomatoes, shredded cabbage, a generous drizzle of garlic oil, and a pinch of dried shrimp.

1. Fermented Tea Leaves
2. Roasted Peanuts
3. Garlic Oil
4. Fresh Tomatoes

Exclusive Comfort Secret

Our exclusive, closely-guarded recipe featuring a perfect blend of rich, comforting flavors.

  • The Foundation

  • The Secret Blend

Garlic Butter Steak

Premium Grill Classic

Premium beef cut elevated with crushed garlic, butter, and high heat.

  • The Sear

    Heat a cast-iron skillet until smoking. Sear the heavily salted prime cut to develop a deep, beautifully caramelized crust.

  • The Baste

    Lower the heat, drop in a generous block of butter, crushed garlic cloves, and fresh thyme. Continually spoon the foaming butter over the meat.

  • The Rest

    Crucially, let the steak rest on a board for at least 10 minutes before slicing to lock in the savory juices.

1. Prime Beef Cut
2. Butter Block
3. Crushed Garlic Cloves
